Wireless Communications • RF Systems • Signal Processing

I build systems that turn signals into insight.

I’m Tanay Agrawal, an M.S. Electrical Engineering student at USC and a researcher in the WiDeS Group. My work spans mmWave channel sounding, OTFS channel estimation, underwater communications, and multi-mode fiber modeling.

See projects LinkedIn
Current
USC WiDeS Group
Research on mmWave sounding and wideband channel characterization under Prof. Andreas Molisch.
Focus
RF + DSP + Optical Systems
Designing hardware-aware algorithms for communication and sensing systems.
Toolkit
MATLAB, Python, C/C++, Verilog, GNU Radio, LTSpice, Keysight ADS, CST Studio Suite, STM32 firmware, VNA, anechoic chamber, oscilloscopes, phased arrays, GPSDO.

About

I am interested in the intersection of wireless communications, signal processing, and hardware systems. My recent work includes distributed mmWave channel sounding, pilot-coded OTFS channel estimation, underwater communications, and linear channel modeling for multi-mode fibers.

Education

University of Southern California
